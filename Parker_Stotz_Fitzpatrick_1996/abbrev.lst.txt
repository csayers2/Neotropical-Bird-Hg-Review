              Codes used in databases

See chapter 12 for details of most of these codes; habitats are described
and defined in chapter 2.

Codes for databases A, C, D, and E (files adata.txt, cdata.txt, ddata.txt and
edata.txt):

Snst  - Sensitivity [2]
  L - Low; M - Medium; H - High.

Stat  - Status [1]
  A - All; F - Fraction; S - Some; T - Total.

Strat  - Foraging Strata  [3]
  T - Terrestrial; U - Understory; M - Midstory; C - Canopy; A - Aerial;
  W - Water. 

Cntab - Center of Abundance [5]
  LT - Lower tropical; LS - Lower subtropical; HT - Hill tropical; UT -
  Upper tropical; US - Upper subtropical; MM - Middle montane; UM - 
  Upper  montane.

Rel - Relative Abundance [3]
  R - Rare; U - Uncommon; F - Fairly common; C - Common; P -
  Patchily distributed.

Min - Minimum Elevation [4]
  0 - Lowlands (see intro.use); numeric values in meters.

Qmin - Minimum elevation qualifier [1]
  ? - Uncertain value.   

Max - Maximum Elevation [4]
  0 - Lowlands (see intro.use); numeric values in meters.

Qmax - Maximum elevation qualifier [1]
  ? - Uncertain value; + - species occurs at least to given elevation but
  likely occurs higher.   

CP -Conservation Priority [1]
  1 - Urgent; 2 - High; 3 - Medium; 4 - Low.

RP - Research Priority [1]
  1 - High; 2 - Medium; 3 - Low.

  Rocky outcrops and caves; S - Streamside; V - Vine tangles; B/V -
  Bamboo/vine tangles.
